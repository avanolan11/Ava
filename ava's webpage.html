Jazz Thornton

Mental health activitst/ speaker/ director/ voices of hope founder

Voices of Hope is a non-profit organisation that aims to break the stigma around mental illness, to decrease sucicidal rates and show that change is possible. It was founded in 2014 by Jazz Thornton and Genevieve Mora, both being mental health surviviours they felt the need to voice their past experiences and show others that change is possible. They aim to create and implement change for mental health, while providing hope through the voices of those with previous experiences. They also provide strategies and content with intent to influence global, communal and individual change.

Stop Surviving Start Fighting is a book written by Jazz and it discusses her journey battling mental illness. She first attempted to take her own life at the age of 12. She spent most of her time in physciatric wards and under medical supervision as she battled through the challenges of deperession and anxiety through her teenage years, but yet the attmepts continued. Jazz Thornton has received world wide recognition for her work on advocating for mental health and using the voices of lived experience to provide hope to those who are struggling

Jazz has her own movie called The Girl on the Bridge which talks about how she has overcome her own sucidial struggles and how she was fighting to change how society and the system are dealing with young people like her. test
